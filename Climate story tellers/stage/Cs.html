Summary

In "The Great Adventure of Mo and Friends," a brave mammoth named Mo, a playful dinosaur named Dee, and a curious fish named Sama embark on a thrilling quest to understand the alarming changes happening in their world, including melting ice and rising oceans. Guided by the wisdom of the Wind Spirit, they learn about climate change and its devastating effects on their environment. Motivated to make a difference, they rally their community to take action by planting trees, cleaning rivers, and promoting sustainability. This engaging story not only entertains but also educates children on the importance of protecting our planet for future generations.

The Last Glacial Maximum (c. 26,500 - 19,000 BCE)

  • B.C.E.

    Characteristics

    • Cold Climate: The Last Glacial Maximum (LGM) featured extensive ice sheets covering large parts of North America, Europe, and Asia.
    • Lower Sea Levels: Sea levels were significantly lower due to the vast amounts of water trapped in ice.

  • B.C.E.

    Causes

    • Milankovitch Cycles: Variations in Earthâ€™s orbit and axial tilt led to significant cooling and glaciation.
    • Atmospheric Conditions: Lower greenhouse gas concentrations contributed to colder temperatures.

  • B.C.E.

    Impacts

    • Human Migration: Humans adapted to colder conditions, with populations migrating to warmer areas as ice sheets retreated.
    • Flora and Fauna: Many species, including megafauna like mammoths and saber-toothed cats, thrived in cold environments but faced extinction as the climate warmed.
